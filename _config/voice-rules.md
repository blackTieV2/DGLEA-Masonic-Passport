# Voice Rules

## Tone

- Plain, supportive, mentoring language.
- Avoid punitive compliance language for normal learning progress.
- Use respectful Masonic terminology without revealing restricted content.

## Warnings

Every input that could accept free text should carry a warning:

> Record only high-level mentoring progress, dates, attendance, reflections, and administrative notes. Do not enter restricted ritual wording, passwords, signs, grips, detailed ritual answers, health details, political views, or other unnecessary personal information.

## Status language

Use consistent status chips across the UI:

- Not started
- Draft
- Submitted
- Clarification requested
- Verified
- Rejected
- Completed
- Locked

Use consistent sync indicators where applicable:

- Synced
- Pending upload
- Conflict
- Failed sync

## Design considerations

- Design for older users: clear contrast, larger tap targets, minimal nested menus, readable typography.
- Do not use icon-only actions for critical workflows.
- Make the journey visible: future stages may be shown as a locked roadmap.
- Readiness recommendations must be clearly labelled as recommendations, not automatic decisions.
